## Slide outline

### 1. The problem

- Shopping is split across stores and private sellers.
- The displayed price is often not the delivered price.
- Used goods add missing evidence and negotiation work.
- One unsafe channel change can erase buyer protection.

### 2. The product

- Conversational shopping agent.
- Search, landed-cost calculation, evidence gathering and seller communication.
- User-defined shopping, negotiation and payment mandate.
- Safe delegated action with explicit stop rules.

### 3. Three moments

- adidas Samba: verified retail purchase within a hard cap.
- Nintendo Switch OLED: evidence request and protected OLX negotiation.
- Fujifilm X100F: simulated seller call and rejected off-platform transfer.

### 4. Trust and scale

- AP2-aligned mandate concept; no compliance claim.
- Local payment UX such as simulated BLIK authorization.
- Complete decision trail and transaction policy.
- Future merchant, marketplace and logistics adapters.
- Von Halsky as a market benchmark, not a partner or integration.

## Two-minute Q&A anchors

- **What is real?** Public product/listing snapshots and deterministic calculations; all communication, calls and transactions are simulated.
- **Why OpenAI?** Natural-language intent, evidence synthesis and bounded agent behavior are the product layer; deterministic fixtures keep the stage demo reliable.
- **Why Solidgate?** The experience makes delegated purchasing measurable and controlled through mandate limits, landed cost and decision receipts; PSP integration is not required by the case.
- **What prevents unsafe purchases?** Hard caps, protected-payment rules, approval gates, stop conditions and auditable reasons.
